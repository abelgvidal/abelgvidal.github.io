Be wrong in Probability Like a Pro “We made too many wrong mistakes”. Yogi Berra. In her book “Randomness,” Deborah Bennett presents a fascinating example that illustrates how bad we are at calculating probabilities, even highly trained professionals. A group of doctors received this question: The test of a disease has a 5% false positive rate. The disease affects 1 in 1000 people in the population.
